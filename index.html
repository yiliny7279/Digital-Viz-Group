WHAT IS A DIGITAL NOMAD



Development and Definitional Distinctions of Digital Nomads

What is a digital nomad?

Makimoto and Manners (1997) proposed the concept of "digital nomads" in their book "Digital Nomad".

“The term “digital nomad” describes people who no longer rely on work in a conventional office; instead, they can decide freely when and where to work. They can essentially work anywhere, as long as they have their laptop with them and access to a good internet connection. This means that their workplace might be in a shared office in Berlin, Germany for one month, and a month later they will be working on the same project in a café in Chiang Mai, Thailand.”

The "digital nomads" proposed in the book are persons who use digital tools and Internet technology to travel the world for work. They will no longer be constrained by geography and will be able to execute work assignments efficiently from anywhere. The book investigates the impact of technology on work and lifestyle, predicting a future in which individuals will work remotely using mobile devices, breaking free from the confines of a fixed workplace.

Reichenberger (2018) explores the idea of digital nomads pursuing total freedom, focusing on their experiences in work and leisure. Reichenberger believes that digital nomads pursue not only freedom of location, but also freedom of lifestyle and career choices.

Digital nomads VS remote workers and expats

To best illustrate the distinction between digital nomads, remote workers, and expats, consider Ina Reichenberger's scalar definition of the term digital nomad in the article Digital Nomads — a quest for holistic freedom in work and leisure:

“Digital nomads are individuals who achieve location independence by:

  • Conducting their work in an online environment,
  • Transferring this independence to mobility by not consistently working in one designated personal office space, but using the possibility to simultaneously work and travel to the extent that no permanent residence exists.”

As a result, unlike remote workers, which is a broader idea, the phrase digital nomad indicates the aspect of travel. In comparison to expats, digital nomads do not seek permanent residence or work for a company registered in the country where they are staying.

Digital Nomad's London Guide

"Digital Nomad's London Guide" is a feature designed specifically for digital nomads, aimed at helping them quickly and easily understand the rental rates across London's boroughs and choose the best place to live. And feature offers a unique "15-Minute Living Zone" feature, which shows the amenities within a 15-minute walk or bike radius from the user's current or chosen location, such as supermarkets and workspaces.

15min zone city

The "15-Minute City Zone" feature provides digital nomads with a powerful tool to visualize and access essential amenities such as supermarkets and offices within a 15-minute walk or bike ride from any given location. This function enhances urban living by promoting convenience, and supporting a more sustainable lifestyle, making it easier for users to integrate work and daily necessities seamlessly into their busy lives.

EPILOGUE

In today's world of globalisation and rapid development of information technology, digital nomads have become an increasingly popular way of working and living. Their presence adds to the cultural diversity and innovation of cities and injects new vigour into local economies.

London offers digital nomads a wide range of flexible and connected workspaces, creative spaces, places to live and convenient living infrastructure, which not only ensures productivity but also provides inspiration and technical support.

London should also respond positively to the Digital Development Strategy 2024-2030 and The London Plan 2021 to strengthen its digital infrastructure and affordable housing; the Mayor is also pushing closer to Tube network coverage to improve network efficiency (Guardian, 22/06/2021).

The story of London's adaptation to this modern work culture has only just begun, and presents a promising prospect for digital nomads seeking both old-world glamour and new-world convenience.
